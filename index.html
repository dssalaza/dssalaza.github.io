Hi, I'm David Salazar

Software Quality Analyst

Have you found asking yourself how does this thing works? That is mostly me, I was probably six-years old when I saw a computer for the first time and since then I fell in love with tech.
I discovered that programming was a thing for me when I was in high school but I decided to shift my carrer into something that mixes hardware and software, that is why I studied telematics engineering. However, as many of us in the IT industry I also suffered of something called "The impostor syndrome" but sooner than later I found out that I was the only one limiting myself.
Ever since I've been trying to broaden my understanding on software development, I've worked as a Software Developer, Database and Network administrator over the past years.
Nevertheless, being a Quality Analyst has thought me so much about achieving higher standards of software products and processes. I have been working on "shifting quality left" and embedding quality into the whole software development lifecycle, I truly believe that testing does not improve quality, identify risks in early stages does. In this way I have ensured robust and fast delivery of an overall high quality product.
